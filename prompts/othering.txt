Out-group othering: language that systematically demonises, dehumanises, or applies generalised negative labels to any out-group. This includes attributing destructive qualities to an out-group, blaming them for misfortunes, treating all members of the outgroup as equally culpable, framing them as hostile or threatening, or describing them as less than human.

# Rules
Label 1 if the text clearly shows out-group othering toward any group. This includes:
- Generalised negative labels or slurs applied to an entire group (based on ethnicity, race, religion, gender, sexuality, etc.)
- Dehumanising language: comparing group members to animals, or using terms like "creature," "vermin," "pest," "breed," "tame"
- Demonising language: describing the group as "evil," "depraved," "enemies," "traitors," "conspirators," "invaders," or attributing other morally reprehensible qualities
- Blaming an out-group for personal or collective misfortunes
- Mocking, celebrating, or gloating over the out-group's suffering or defeats
- Mentions of "14" or "14 words" that could indicate "We must secure the existence of our people and a future for white children"

Label 0 for:
- Criticism of specific individuals, policies, or actions without generalising to an entire group
- Neutral reporting or descriptive statements about events
- Counter-speech condemning hatred or defending the rights of the outgroup
- Ambiguous or mixed messages that do not clearly convey othering
- Generic violent, aggressive, or obscene language that does not target a specific identity group (e.g., insults directed at an individual without referencing their group membership)
- Cultural or media commentary without demonising a group
When in doubt or ambiguous, label 0.

# Examples
Text: "I want to burn this whole place to the ground"
Label: 0
Reason: Violent and aggressive, but no identity group is targeted or generalised about.

Text: "bro you're tripping, sit down fool"
Label: 0
Reason: Slang, casual insults, or profanity directed at an individual in informal speech — even if crude or offensive — are not out-group othering unless they systematically demonise or dehumanise an identity group.

Text: "We stand tall and proud as one people. Glory to our heritage."
Label: 0
Reason: In-group pride and identity celebration. Does not demonise, dehumanise, or generalise negatively about any out-group.

Text: "You're a pathetic loser and a disgrace, go to hell"
Label: 0
Reason: Personal insult directed at an individual, not generalising to any group.

Text: "They are all parasites leeching off our country, every single one of them"
Label: 1
Reason: Dehumanising label ("parasites") applied to an entire group with generalisation ("every single one").

# Output
Return ONLY the numeric value, with no extra text:
0 = No out-group othering detected
1 = Out-group othering detected
