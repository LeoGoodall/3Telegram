Violence condoning norms: language that calls for, announces, justifies, or glorifies violence. This includes explicit calls for killing, harm, or destruction, as well as legitimising violence as necessary, unavoidable, defensive, pre-emptive, or morally required. It also includes glorifying perpetrators of violence as heroes, martyrs, or defenders.

# Rules
Label 1 if the text clearly condones violence. This includes:
- Explicit calls for harm, killing, or destruction directed at people or groups
- Announcing intentions to commit violence
- Justifying violence as self-defence, protection, pre-emptive action, or escape from a harmful condition
- Claiming that peaceful alternatives to violence would be ineffective
- Language such as "pre-emptive," "forced to fight," "need for war," "protect our people"
- Calling for war, retaliation, or punishment by violent means
- Glorifying perpetrators of violence, framing them as heroes, martyrs, saints, or defenders
- Language that reveals fixation on specific weapons (e.g. extensive focus on specific gun models, ammunition or explosives)
- Any mentions of the number 88
- Euphemistic or indirect endorsements of violence — e.g., describing people as a "target rich environment," calling for groups to be "purged" or "cleansed," or expressing "no remorse" for violence against specific groups
- "RAHOWA" (abbreviation for "Racial Holy War") and "DOTR" (abbreviation for "Day of the Rope," referencing mass executions in The Turner Diaries) — these are explicit calls for violence even though they appear as abbreviations

Label 0 for:
- Mere descriptions of violent events without endorsement
- Calls for justice, legal accountability, or punishment without advocating violence
- Non-violent criticism or hostility toward any group
- Ambiguous metaphors that do not clearly endorse or celebrate violence
- Insults, slurs, or profanity that express hostility but do not genuinely call for, justify, or glorify physical violence (e.g., "drop dead" or "go to hell" as expressions of contempt)
- Reporting or quoting others' violent statements without endorsing them
- Slang where violent-sounding words are used non-literally (e.g., "nuke the server" meaning shut it down)
- Extreme racial slurs or hateful epithets that express bigotry but do not specifically call for, justify, or glorify physical violence — the presence of slurs alone does not constitute violence condoning
When in doubt or ambiguous, label 0.

# Examples
Text: "You worthless scum, I hope you rot. I'm gonna nuke their channel"
Label: 0
Reason: Hostile insult expressing contempt, but not a genuine call for, justification of, or glorification of violence. Nuking often refers to shutting a Telegram channel down, not violence.

Text: "A senior official reportedly said all dissenters should be eliminated"
Label: 0
Reason: Reporting someone else's violent statement, not endorsing or condoning it.

Text: "RAHOWA is inevitable. No mercy for the enemy."
Label: 1
Reason: "RAHOWA" is an abbreviation for "Racial Holy War" — an explicit call for race war. Combined with "no mercy for the enemy," this condones and glorifies violence.

Text: "It looks like he suffered massive internal injuries and bled out within minutes"
Label: 0
Reason: Clinical or factual description of injuries or a violent event, without endorsing, celebrating, or calling for violence.

Text: "The only solution is armed struggle. Every fighter who takes up the cause is a hero and a martyr for our people."
Label: 1
Reason: Explicitly calls for armed violence and glorifies those who carry it out as heroes and martyrs.

# Output
Return ONLY the numeric value, with no extra text:
0 = No violence condoning language detected
1 = Violence condoning language detected
